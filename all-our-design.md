## 1. Aesthetic Essence 🎨

The GATHER look is built to make an everyday product feel like a reason for people to come together. It is the opposite of cold, corporate, and clinical.

**Five pillars:**
1. **Dark and moody base** — content lives on deep near-black backgrounds, not white.
2. **Vibrant, single-focus accents** — one bold color does the heavy lifting per layout.
3. **Warm and nostalgic** — modern execution, but it feels like something you grew up with.
4. **Human and unfussy** — plain language, real moments, no jargon, no gloss.
5. **Breathing room** — one idea per screen, generous space, confident restraint.

**Adjective set to feed any AI tool:** *warm, nostalgic, community-driven, anti-corporate, bold, candid, relaxed, confident, inclusive, modern-retro.*

## 2. Brand Personality ✏️🎨

**Reusable archetype (keep):** primarily **The Everyman** (belonging, relatability) with a streak of **The Lover** (connection, shared moments). The brand behaves like the friend who gets everyone together and keeps it real.

**Reusable trait set (keep):**
- Warm, not slick
- Inclusive — built for everyone, not an elite
- Nostalgic but modern
- Honest / anti-corporate
- Energetic with a little irreverence
- Present and accountable (a real human answers)

## 3. Voice & Tone 🎨 (mechanics, not copy)

Keep the *mechanics* below; write your own lines.

**Voice principles:**
- **Short, punchy sentences.** Fragments encouraged. Stack three short beats for rhythm.
- **Second person + collective "we / our."** Pull the reader into the group.
- **Plain language.** No buzzwords, no corporate filler. Say it like a person.
- **Confident swagger on product lines** — one tight, sensory, slightly cocky line per item.
- **A little friendly edge** is on-brand. Don't over-sanitize.
- **Warm and accountable** in service/support contexts.

**Naming convention to reuse 🎨:** prefix collective nouns to build belonging (e.g. an "Our [X]" pattern for nav and programs — a community/club name instead of "membership," a "shop" rename instead of "products").

**Tone by context:**

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Product / offer descriptions | Bold, sensory, one tight line, a little swagger |
| About / story | Warm, sincere, nostalgic |
| Community / CTA | Inviting, inclusive, conversational |
| Support | Human, accountable, responsive |
| Sales / B2B | Keep the warmth, add proof, never go stiff |

**Do / Don't:**
- ✅ Three short beats: "Thing in hand. People around. Moments that matter."
- ❌ "Our premium solution facilitates engagement."
- ✅ "Real ingredients. Way less filler."
- ❌ "Crafted with an artisanal blend of curated components."

## 4. Color Palette 🎨🔎

The structure *is* the theme: **dark base → off-white text → vibrant accents.** Swap exact hex to taste, but keep the relationships.

### 4.1 Core palette (keep the roles)

| Role | Swatch name | Default hex 🔎 | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base / background | Ink | `#11110F` | Primary dark backgrounds, heroes |
| Surface | Charcoal | `#1E1E1C` | Cards, secondary panels |
| Primary text on dark | Off-White | `#FAF7F2` | Body + headlines on dark |
| Pure white | White | `#FFFFFF` | Logo lockup only — keeps logo the brightest thing |

### 4.2 Accent set ✏️🔎
Pick **one to three** vibrant accents. The original used a warm-to-bold spread (a golden-warm, a bright, and a deep bold). Reuse that range.

| Accent | Swatch name | Default hex 🔎 | Mood |
|---|---|---|---|
| Accent 1 | Golden Hour | `#F4A24C` | Warm, inviting |
| Accent 2 | Bright | `#F2C53D` | Punchy, energetic |
| Accent 3 | Bold | `#C0344E` | Deep, confident, attitude |

### 4.3 Usage rules 🎨
- **Default canvas is dark.** Build web, social, and slides on the dark base.
- **One accent per layout.** Don't rainbow all accents together except in a full-lineup shot.
- **Off-white for text, pure white only for the logo.**
- **Accents = energy, not walls.** Use on CTAs, highlights, underlines, tags. Keep big fields dark.
- **Contrast:** every text/background pair ≥ 4.5:1 (check with a free contrast checker). Accent-on-dark for large text only.

## 5. Typography 🎨🔎

### 5.1 Font roles (pick fonts, keep the pattern)

| Role | Pick a font ✏️ | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Display / Headlines | `[DISPLAY FONT]` | Bold, characterful |
| Body | `[BODY FONT]` | Clean, readable |
| Accent / labels | `[LABEL FONT]` or reuse display | For uppercase eyebrows |

**Signature pattern to keep 🎨:** small **UPPERCASE eyebrow label → large bold headline → one short line → single CTA.** This micro-eyebrow rhythm is core to the look.

### 5.2 Type scale 🔎

| Token | Use | Size | Weight | Case | Tracking |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Eyebrow | Section micro-label | ~12–14px | 600 | UPPERCASE | wide (+0.1em) |
| H1 / Hero | Page hero | ~48–72px | 700 | Title/Sentence | tight |
| H2 | Section title | ~32–40px | 700 | — | normal |
| H3 | Subsection / item name | ~20–24px | 600 | often UPPERCASE | normal |
| Body | Paragraphs | ~16–18px | 400 | Sentence | normal |
| Small | Meta / captions | ~13–14px | 400 | — | normal |
| Button | CTA label | ~14–16px | 600 | often UPPERCASE | slight |

### 5.3 Rules 🎨
- Headlines short and declarative — match the fragmented voice.
- Generous body line-height (1.5–1.7) for a relaxed feel.
- Uppercase only for eyebrows, item names, and buttons — never long passages.

## 6. Logo Usage 🎨✏️

- **Primary lockup:** a **white** wordmark/mark, used on dark or accent fields. ✏️ Add your own.
- Never white logo on light. Always ensure strong contrast.
- **Clear space:** padding ≥ the cap-height of the wordmark's first letter.
- **Don't:** recolor, add effects, stretch, or place on busy photos without a dark scrim.
- **Always have:** white version + a dark/black version + a transparent SVG (for light collateral and slides).

## 7. Spacing & Layout 🎨🔎

### 7.1 Spacing scale 🔎

| Token | Value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| space-xs | 4–8px | Inline gaps |
| space-sm | 12–16px | Within components |
| space-md | 24–32px | Between elements |
| space-lg | 48–64px | Between subsections |
| space-xl | 96–120px | Between major sections (let heroes breathe) |

### 7.2 Layout patterns 🎨
- **Vertical, story-driven scroll** — full-width stacked sections, one idea each.
- **Values as a rhythmic single-word list** — each its own beat.
- **Item trio** — three offers in parallel, each with a one-line sensory description + CTA.
- **Big breathing room**, centered hero-led composition, logo as anchor.

**Reusable section recipe 🎨:** `UPPERCASE eyebrow → bold statement → one short line → single CTA`, on a dark field. Works for web sections, slides, and one-sheeters alike.

---

## 8. Components 🎨🔎

### Buttons
- Labels are action-driven and short; often UPPERCASE.
- 🔎 Define: fill (accent or off-white), text color, radius (sharp vs pill), padding, and **hover state**.

### Item / product card
- Sensory eyebrow line → item name (UPPERCASE) → CTA.
- Define: image treatment, card background, spacing, hover behavior.

### Navigation
- Short labels, reuse the collective-noun prefix pattern.
- Define: sticky vs static, background, mobile menu style.

### Forms
- Keep minimal: Name · Email · Message · Send.
- Define: input borders, focus state, button style.

> For each component record: dimensions, colors (ref §4), type (ref §5), radius, shadow, and all states (default / hover / active / disabled).

## 9. Imagery & Art Direction 🎨

- **Mood:** real life, real people, shared moments — lifestyle over sterile studio.
- **Subject:** gatherings, friends, hands-on-product, everyday-but-meaningful moments. Multi-generational and inclusive.
- **Treatment:** warm, natural, lightly nostalgic / film-toned.
- **Product shots:** clean render on the dark base.
- **Do:** authentic candid energy, hands holding product, groups.
- **Don't:** stocky corporate imagery, isolated perfection, cold/clinical lighting.

---

## 10. Iconography 🔎
- Minimal and friendly — simple line icons.
- Keep a consistent stroke weight.
- Document the set once chosen.

---

## 11. Motion & Interaction 🎨🔎
- Section reveals: gentle fade/slide-in on scroll.
- Button hovers: soft color shift, ~150–250ms ease.
- Drawers/menus: easy slide-in.
- **Principle:** motion feels relaxed and effortless, never flashy.

## 12. Application Guidelines 🎨✏️

**Web:** dark base, section recipe (§7.2), one accent per region, lifestyle imagery, short punchy headlines.

**Branding:** lead with value words + a short rally line ✏️. White logo on dark. Warm-nostalgic, anti-corporate.

**Sales collateral / one-sheeters:** dark background, white logo top, eyebrow → bold claim → short proof line → CTA. Keep the human voice; add proof without going stiff.

**Social:** one accent per post; short caption in brand voice (fragments, swagger); UPPERCASE item names; reuse a consistent tagline slot ✏️.

**Presentations:** one idea per slide; dark template; off-white text; accent for emphasis; big breathing room.

## Appendix A — Adapt this theme to a new brand (10-minute setup)

- ✏️ Fill `[BRAND NAME]`, `[PRODUCT]`, value words (§2), and a short rally line.
- 🔎 Lock your accent set (§4.2): pick 1–3 vibrant colors that suit the product, keeping the warm-to-bold spread.
- ✏️ Choose a display + body font (§5.1); keep the uppercase-eyebrow pattern.
- ✏️ Prepare white + dark + SVG logo versions (§6).
- 🔎 Confirm button radius and hover (§8) — this small choice sets the personality (sharp = modern/edgy, pill = friendly/soft).
- Write copy using the §3 mechanics, in your own words.

## Appendix B — Extracting these values from any reference site (Chrome DevTools)

Use this when you want to pull exact specs from *any* site you admire to feed this template:
- **Color:** right-click element → **Inspect** → **Styles** panel → read `color` / `background-color` hex (click the swatch to copy exact).
- **Font:** right-click headline → **Inspect** → **Computed** tab → **Rendered Fonts** at the bottom names the real font; also note `font-size`, `font-weight`, `letter-spacing`, `text-transform`.
- **Spacing:** Inspect → read the **box model** diagram for exact margin/padding px.
- **Hover state:** in **Styles**, click **:hov** → tick `:hover` to freeze and read it.
- **Radius/shadow:** read `border-radius` and `box-shadow` in **Styles**.
- **Color from an image:** save it → drop into a free image color-picker (or ColorZilla).
